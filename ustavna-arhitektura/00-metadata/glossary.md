# Glossary of Terms

This document explains key diving terms used throughout the Handbook and Web App.

- **Decompression Stop:** A pause at a specific depth to allow excess nitrogen to safely leave the body.  
- **Dive Tables:** Charts used to plan dives and avoid decompression sickness.  
- **Gas Mixture:** The composition of gases (O2, N2, He) in a diver’s tank.  
- **Pressure Physics:** Study of how pressure changes underwater and its effects on gases and the human body.  
- **Redundancy:** Backup equipment or systems to ensure safety during dives.  
- **Self-Reflection:** Analyzing one’s own decisions and actions after each dive to improve skills and safety.  
- **Situational Assessment:** Evaluating the environment, conditions, and risks before and during a dive.  
- **Team Diving:** Diving with one or more partners with coordinated communication and safety protocols.  
- **Nitrogen Loading:** Accumulation of nitrogen in body tissues during a dive.  
- **Oxygen Toxicity:** Harmful effects of breathing high concentrations of oxygen under pressure.
